About The artist

Stanisław Szukalski was a Polish sculptor and painter who became a part of the Chicago Renaissance. Wikipedia Born: December 13, 1893, Warta, Poland Died: May 19, 1987 (age 93 years), Burbank, California, United States Movies: Struggle: The Life and Lost Art of Szukalski Children: Elzbieta Kalina Szukalski Spouse: Joan Lee Donovan (m. 1934–1980), Helen Walker (m. 1922–1932)

Stanisław Szukalski (13 December 1893 – 19 May 1987) was a Polish sculptor and painter associated with the Chicago Renaissance. His art drew inspiration from ancient cultures like Egyptian, Slavic, and Aztec, as well as art nouveau and various early 20th-century European modernist movements. During the 1920s, he was celebrated as Poland's "greatest living artist," known for his style called "Bent Classicism." Additionally, Szukalski developed the pseudoscientific-historical theory of Zermatism, proposing that all human culture originated from post-deluge Easter Island and that humanity was engaged in an eternal struggle with the Sons of Yeti ("Yetinsyny"), the offspring of Yeti and humans.

Szukalski's post-1940 work focused on unraveling prehistoric mysteries, including the origins of language, religion, and culture. He claimed to find Polish roots for various ancient phenomena, detailed in his extensive 42-volume manuscript called Protong. Additionally, he proposed Zermatism, a theory suggesting that all human culture originated from post-deluge Easter Islanders settling in Zermatt. According to Szukalski, humanity has been locked in an eternal struggle with the Sons of Yeti, believed to be the offspring of Yeti and humans. Despite lacking scientific credibility, his theories gained a following due to his artistic illustrations.

Bray and Lena Zwalve oversee Szukalski's estate, known as "Archives Szukalski." They published "The Lost Tune: Early Works (1913-1930)" in 1990. Szukalski's admirers include Leonardo DiCaprio, who sponsored a retrospective at the Laguna Art Museum in 2000. His works are displayed at the Polish Museum of America in Chicago. Despite WWII losses, his legacy endures with exhibitions worldwide. In 2018, DiCaprio produced the Netflix documentary "Struggle: The Life and Lost Art of Szukalski."
